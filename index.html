Essential Financial Principles

Master these fundamental principles for better financial health

50/30/20 Rule
  • 50% - Needs (housing, food, utilities)
  • 30% - Wants (entertainment, dining out)
  • 20% - Savings & Investments
Emergency Fund
  • Save 3-6 months of expenses
  • Keep in high-yield savings account
  • Only use for true emergencies
  • Replenish after use
Compound Interest
  • Start investing early
  • Reinvest dividends
  • Time is your greatest ally
  • Small amounts grow significantly over time
Diversification
  • Don't put all eggs in one basket
  • Mix stocks, bonds, real estate
  • Consider international exposure
  • Rebalance periodically
Smart Spending Tips
Track Every Penny

Use this tool to monitor all expenses and identify spending patterns.

Wait 24 Hours

For non-essential purchases over $100, wait 24 hours before buying.

Pay Yourself First

Automatically save 20% of income before spending on anything else.

Risk Management Fundamentals
Risk Assessment
  • Low Risk - Government bonds, savings accounts
  • Medium Risk - Blue-chip stocks, index funds
  • High Risk - Cryptocurrency, individual stocks
Risk Mitigation
  • Diversify across asset classes
  • Maintain emergency fund
  • Use stop-loss orders
  • Regular portfolio rebalancing
Asset Allocation by Age
  • 20s-30s: 80% stocks, 20% bonds
  • 40s-50s: 60% stocks, 40% bonds
  • 60s+: 40% stocks, 60% bonds
Common Risk Mistakes
  • Investing without emergency fund
  • Putting all money in one stock
  • Timing the market
  • Ignoring inflation risk
